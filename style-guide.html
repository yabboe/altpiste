Understanding Student Needs for Digital Service Innovation

From research insights, we developed low-fidelity wireframes focused on students' critical needs. This established an intuitive class registration flow, course search, schedule building, and clear payment processes with centralized resource access. These foundational wireframes evolved into clickable prototypes for usability testing, ensuring key features remained accessible within two taps

miranda, 17 - high Schooler

Key Needs: Guidance on college applications, financial aid, and accessible transit.

Pain Points: Lack of career path clarity, confusion about applications, financial barriers.

Caleb 18 - Enrolled Freshman

Key Needs: Navigating campus, managing finances, tech-forward tools for daily tasks.

Pain Points: College costs, difficulty finding campus resources, financial planning.

Olivia 23 - Nontraditional Student

Key Needs: Efficient tools, childcare support, simple financial planning, and academic guidance.

Pain Points: Limited time, hard-to-find resources, financial pressure, and heavy workload.

System Audit & Key Findings

outdated mobile experience
Past university apps failed by not solving real student problems. Partnering with UVU’s Digital Transformation team and Student Council, we validated a key opportunity: a unified platform for schedules, deadlines, and alerts—a feature prospective students now expect from tech-forward institutions.
disjointed desktop applications
UVU’s desktop systems were fragmented across multiple platforms, requiring students to jump between interfaces to register, view schedules, and contact advisors. The experience was inefficient, unintuitive, and often frustrating.
Competitor App Review
Analysis of eight peer universities showed outdated, fragmented apps. By contrast, UVU’s solution would set a new standard, combining best practices (e.g., integrated calendars) with unique differentiators (e.g., personalized alerts)—a selling point for enrollment and engagement.
administrative overhead
With 40% of support tickets tied to password issues, streamlining login processes not only reduces staff workload but also improves first impressions for students, aligning with UVU’s commitment to accessibility and cutting-edge service.
04 / visual design
A Lightweight Student First Design
Building upon foundational research and iterative wireframing, our visual design phase transformed functional prototypes into a refined, engaging, and intuitive digital experience. We prioritized clarity, consistency, and accessibility, ensuring the interface not only looked appealing but also facilitated seamless user interaction and strengthened the UVU brand. Every visual decision aimed to enhance usability, reduce cognitive load, and create a modern aesthetic that resonates with UVU's diverse student body, delivering a familiar yet fresh product.
Design System & Principles
System Icons
A consistent and intuitive icon set, meticulously refined for mobile screens, ensures clear visual communication and a seamless user experience across the application.
Brand & System Colors
Adapted from existing UVU marketing colors, this palette is carefully curated for brand consistency and ensures AA compliance for optimal contrast and readability in both light and dark modes.
Core UI Components
These foundational components, including interactive accordions and the digital ID, provide a consistent user experience designed to reduce cognitive load and simplify complex information.
