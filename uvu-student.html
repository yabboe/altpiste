The UVU Student Mobile App project addressed a critical challenge for Utah Valley University, a large institution with over 44,000 students: a fragmented and outdated digital ecosystem that burdened students with disconnected systems for essential tasks. As Creative Lead, I directed a team of three designers to reimagine UVU's digital experience, designing an intuitive mobile interface that unified key services like tuition payments and class registration. Our solution transformed institutional pain points into a seamless student journey, setting a new benchmark for digital innovation at UVU.

System Audit & Key Findings

outdated mobile experience

Past university apps failed by not solving real student problems. Partnering with UVU’s Digital Transformation team and Student Council, we validated a key opportunity: a unified platform for schedules, deadlines, and alerts—a feature prospective students now expect from tech-forward institutions.

Fragmented Desktop Ecosystem

UVU's existing desktop systems were fragmented across multiple platforms, requiring students to create separate logins for essential services like Greenbucks, scheduling, and financial aid, which led to significant frustration. The university sought a holistic solution that would provide a single login to access everything students need in their academic life.

Competitor App Review

Analysis of eight peer universities showed outdated, fragmented apps. By contrast, UVU’s solution would set a new standard, combining best practices (e.g., integrated calendars) with unique differentiators (e.g., personalized alerts)—a selling point for enrollment and engagement.

administrative overhead

With 40% of support tickets tied to password issues, streamlining login processes not only reduces staff workload but also improves first impressions for students, aligning with UVU’s commitment to accessibility and cutting-edge service.

Understanding Student Needs for Digital Service Innovation

From research insights, we developed low-fidelity wireframes focused on students' critical needs. This established an intuitive class registration flow, course search, schedule building, and clear payment processes with centralized resource access. These foundational wireframes evolved into clickable prototypes for usability testing, ensuring key features remained accessible within two taps.

Low-Fidelity Wireframes  
& Interactive Prototypes

We began by turning research insights into low-fidelity wireframes that prioritized students’ most critical tasks. Class registration included intuitive course search and a visual schedule builder; tuition payments highlighted deadlines and progress.

Campus resources were centralized for quick access. Through multiple iterations, these wireframes formed the app’s foundation and were refined into clickable prototypes, ensuring key features stayed within two taps on any screen.

testing & iteration

Usability testing with 30 students revealed opportunities to streamline complex processes. We simplified payment screens, enhanced visibility of scheduling conflicts, and refined terminology based on direct feedback gathered from participants during these testing sessions. These iterations proved successful - final testing showed task completion rates improving from 61% to 85%, with particularly strong results in registration and payment workflows. The process demonstrated how iterative testing could transform initial concepts into effective solutions.

Refining the Student Experience: From Complexity to Clarity

navigating complexity

Users struggled with the original navigation's inconsistent naming conventions ("Register" vs. "Schedule") and an excessive number of semester options. This led to confusion, backtracking, and difficulty finding the correct information, particularly for the registration process.

the solution

To address these issues, we simplified the navigation by reducing options by 60%, renaming actions to match student mental models, and aligning flows with academic deadlines. Follow-up testing confirmed a more intuitive experience, with click paths shortening by 40% and frustration signals disappearing.
